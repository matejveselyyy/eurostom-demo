Pure typographic divider — line + mark + line.

The phone number, given proper editorial weight.

Option D · Nothing

Just delete the marquee. Let sections breathe.

The Visit section already has section padding. Adding a divider for "visual breathing" might be padding what's already padding. Sometimes the cleanest move is removing decoration and letting the sections sit next to each other. Pricing has its own bg-color (cream), Visit has its own (bg-warm). The color shift alone signals the transition.

Recommendation.

My pick: D — Nothing. The Pricing section ends with insurance chips on its own bg, the Visit section starts on a different bg with its own section heading and address. The visual transition is already there via background color and section padding. A divider here would be padding-on-padding.

Strong second: B — Phone CTA Band — but only if you want to give the phone CTA a stronger moment between price and visit. It's the only option that adds a useful action to the page.

Skip A: ornamental rule is the laziest editorial flourish — fine, but adds nothing.

Skip C: chapter dividers work in editorials with consistent chapter structure. EUROSTOM doesn't have that elsewhere on the page (no I., II.) so suddenly inserting "III." would be inconsistent.

If you want to keep things clean Pick D — Nothing
If you want a phone CTA push Pick B — Phone Band
If you want pure ornament Pick A — Rule + ℞
If you want chapter framing Pick C — III. Návštěva
