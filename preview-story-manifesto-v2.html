What changed from v1.

v1 had a fixed reception photo in a 5/4 frame on the right. v2 makes the photo a full-section bleed that occupies 68% of the viewport, with masked edges that dissolve into the cream background on the left, top, and bottom. The frame is no longer a hard rectangle — it's a soft, asymmetric presence.

Five photos cycle as you progress through the five stages, each crossfading over 1400ms with a slow scale (1.04 → 1.0) for a "settling in" feel. Narrative arc:

  • Stage 1: dsc_9340 — daytime exterior, EUROSTOM signage on the modern facade — "this is the place"
  • Stage 2: dsc_9436 — wide reception interior — "this is what greets you"
  • Stage 3: dsc_9433 — branded reception desk close-up — "EUROSTOM signage anchors the brand"
  • Stage 4: dsc_9484 — operatory with sink and panoramic monitor — "this is where the work happens"
  • Stage 5: dsc_9420 — waiting room with white sofas and art — "and this is where you wait"

Soft frame: instead of border-radius: 16px with a hard cutoff, the photo uses three mask-image linear gradients that fade to transparent over 18% of the photo's width on the left, plus 14% top and bottom cream-color overlays. No hard edges anywhere except where the photo meets the right viewport edge.

Caption in the bottom-right corner shows current stage number + Czech caption per photo. White type with subtle shadow against the photo. Updates with each stage.

Mobile: photo bleeds full-width with a vertical mask (fades into cream at the bottom), text stacks above. Same crossfade per stage, simpler layout.
